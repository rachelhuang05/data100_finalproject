Our Conclusion

The effects of climate change throughout the entire world have continued to worse as time goes on. This can be especially seen in the state of California from 1975-2023, where increased temperatures have decreased the amount of available land for housing. This is because increased temperatures have been making ice sheets melt faster, and since California is a coastal state, the impact of the resultant rising sea levels on its housing prices is especially apparent. There is less available real estate, so homebuyers are willing to pay more for what is available. This means that sellers are able to raise their prices above what their property is actually worth.

However, this correlation between California's temperature and housing prices should take into account the inflation of the US dollar. The US dollar has inflated by a total of over 600% during this time period of 1975-2023, which means that the dollar amounts of commodities have risen, even if their value has not, because more money is needed to buy the same amount of something has inflation increases.

California's increasing housing prices are causing a housing shortage, which is only one of the many negative effects of climage change. Climate change has already greatly damaged our environment, and by extension, humanity, and if the world is to mitigate the negative effects of climate change in the future and try to slow it down, individuals will have to become more aware of how they consume their resources, so that society as a whole can be kinder to the environment.

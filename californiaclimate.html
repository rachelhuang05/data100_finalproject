Climate Change and Housing Prices in California

Increased temperatures due to climate change lead to:

  • Higher likelihood of forest fires
  • Greater electricity usage
  • Decreased air quality
  • Increased sea levels due to melting glaciers

Increased sea levels decrease the amount of available land for housing; Californiaâ€™s coastal sea levels have increased by almost 8 inches in the past century, and those sea levels are estimated to rise another 20-55 inches by 2100.

Climate change has decreased the amount of available land for housing in California. Thus, people are willing to pay higher prices for housing; as a result, realtors can raise prices as demand increases.
